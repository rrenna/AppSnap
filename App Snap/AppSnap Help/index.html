How to App Snap

While we go into the nuts and bolts of App Snap's settings below, the most important knowledge to get you started is this : the Snap shortcut, ‚å•S , works regardless if App Snap's window is minimized, visible or closed.

As long as App Snap is running, you can keep Snap'ing away. Images in the preview drawer can be dragged into any other application or folder, but don't worry if you forget, all your Snaps are automatically saved in your Snap History. (Window > History)

App Snap's functionality is divided into three sections: iOS, Mac and Android. Below are descriptions of the options in each.

iOS
CommandDescription
Crop SimulatorRemoves the iOS Device graphical element from the rendered image. This should be selected to render iTunes Connect suitable images.
Crop iOS ToolbarRemoves the iOS Toolbar from the rendered image. Apple recommends you don't include a toolbar in your iTunes screenshots. Only avaliable if you've Cropped the iOS Simulator.
Mac
CommandDescription
Use Stock Desktop backgroundThis will replace the your current desktop background with the default Mac desktop background.
Hide Desktop Icons and FinderHides any Desktop Icons and Finder windows open on your screen.
Smart-Transform for iTunes ConnectResizes your Desktop background to an iTunes Connect suitable size, and attempts to move your selected App window inside the new dimensions. This is especially important if you're running App Snap on a super-high resolution monitor, and want to avoid resizing images manually for iTunes Connect. Most importantly is that the app window itself will not be scaled.
Android
CommandDescription
Crop SimulatorRemoves the Android Simulator graphical element from the rendered image.
